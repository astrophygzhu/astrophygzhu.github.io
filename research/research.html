Extragalactic Astrophysics

Active galactic nuclei · Jets · Blazars · Quasars · Supermassive Black Holes · Multi-wavelength Astrophysics

Our research group focuses on the physics of Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) — the energetic cores of galaxies powered by supermassive black holes. We combine multi-wavelength observations, theoretical modeling, and advanced statistical methods to understand their accretion processes, jet formation, and role in galaxy evolution.

What Are Active Galactic Nuclei?

AGN are among the most luminous and persistent sources of electromagnetic radiation in the universe. They are powered by the accretion of matter onto a supermassive black hole (SMBH) at the center of a galaxy. The released gravitational energy can outshine the entire host galaxy, producing radiation across the electromagnetic spectrum — from radio to gamma-rays.

Key Components of an AGN

  • Accretion Disk – A hot, rotating disk of gas and dust feeding the black hole.
  • Relativistic Jet – Collimated outflows of plasma moving at near-light speed.
  • Broad Line Region (BLR) & Narrow Line Region (NLR) – Ionized gas clouds emitting characteristic emission lines.
  • Torus – A dusty structure obscuring the central engine in some orientations.

Our Research Themes

1. Blazars and Relativistic Jets

We study blazars — a subclass of AGN with jets pointing nearly toward Earth — to investigate particle acceleration, magnetic field structures, and Doppler-boosting effects.

2. AGN Variability and Physics of Accretion

By analyzing light curves across wavelengths, we probe the innermost regions of accretion disks, study turbulence and instabilities, and test models of black hole accretion states.

3. AGN in Galaxy Evolution

AGN feedback is a key process shaping galaxy formation. We examine how AGN outflows regulate star formation, enrich the interstellar medium, and influence the growth of their host galaxies.

Methodology & Tools

Our research integrates:

  • Multi-wavelength data analysis (radio to gamma-ray)
  • Time-domain astrophysics (monitoring campaigns, flare studies)
  • Spectral modeling (X-ray spectral fitting, emission line diagnostics)
  • Statistical methods & machine learning for classification and pattern detection
"AGN are not only cosmic powerhouses but also unique laboratories for studying fundamental physics under extreme conditions — from general relativity to plasma astrophysics."
