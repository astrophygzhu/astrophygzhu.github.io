  • A Study of Broad Emission Line and Doppler Factor Estimation for Fermi Blazars

    Chen et. al. 2024
    In this work, we obtained a sample of 979 Fermi blazars from published works and 278 objects from developed three catalogs, the Fermi Large Area Telescope and the Large Sky Area Multi-Object Fiber Spectroscopic Telescope. We estimated the Doppler factor $\delta$, $\gamma$-ray luminosities $L_\gamma$, the synchrotron peak frequency $\nu_p$, and the broad-line region (BLR) luminosity. Our analyses and discussions led to the following main conclusions: For the 278 blazars, their BLR luminosity $\log L_{\mathrm{BLR}}$ ranges from $40.44$ to $45.45~\mathrm{erg~s^{-1}}$, with a mean value of $43.39~\mathrm{erg~s^{-1}}$. The Doppler factor $\delta$ ranges from $0.45$ to $88.52$, with a mean value of $12.99$ for the 979 Fermi blazars, which is consistent with previous results. Both the BLR luminosity and the Doppler factor exhibit positive correlations with the $\gamma$-ray luminosity. The BLR luminosity is anticorrelated with synchrotron peak frequency, suggesting Compton cooling. We derived a relation $\log L_{\mathrm{BLR}} = 1.58 \log \nu_p - 19.46$ that separates BL Lacertae objects and flat-spectrum radio quasars in the $\log L_{\mathrm{BLR}}$ versus $\log \nu_p$ diagram, obtained using a machine-learning method. Based on equivalent width and Doppler factor analysis, we propose five changing-look blazar candidates.

  • Jet Mechanism and γ-Ray-emitting Region for Fermi Flat-spectrum Radio Quasars with Broad-line Emissions

    Zhang et. al. 2024
    Blazars are a subject of intense debate, specifically regarding their jet launch and emission mechanisms, and the origins of their $\gamma$-ray radiation. To explore these issues, we have built a comprehensive sample of flat-spectrum radio quasars (FSRQs) with well-characterized spectral energy distributions. This study aims to elucidate the dominant jet launch mechanism and the main processes behind the inverse Compton (IC) component. Additionally, we seek to pinpoint the location of the $\gamma$-ray dissipation region relative to the central black hole, denoted as $R_{\gamma}$. Our approach involves a detailed analysis of broad-line region (BLR) emission, from which we derive robust estimates of black hole masses using two distinct virial techniques. This enables us to constrain the jet power across a wide array of FSRQs. Our findings lead to several significant conclusions: (I) The correlation between jet power and black hole mass allows us to test the Blandford–Znajek, Blandford–Payne, and hybrid mechanisms. We find that the hybrid mechanism is most effective in explaining the jet power observed in the majority of FSRQs. (II) The IC component of the $\gamma$-rays in FSRQs is predominantly due to the external Compton process. (III) Through simulations, we determine the minimum and maximum values of $R_{\gamma}$ (the $\gamma$-ray dissipation region) and conclude that it is located outside the BLR. This conclusion is derived from variability timescale analysis.
